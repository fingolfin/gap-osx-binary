  [33X[0;0YA  [13Xdivision  ring[113X is a ring (see ChapterÂ [14X56[114X) in which every non-zero element
  has  an  inverse.  The  most  important  class  of  division  rings  are the
  commutative ones, which are called [13Xfields[113X.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0Y[5XGAP[105X  supports  finite fields (see ChapterÂ [14X59[114X) and abelian number fields (see
  ChapterÂ [14X60[114X), in particular the field of rationals (see ChapterÂ [14X17[114X).[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YThis  chapter  describes  the  general [5XGAP[105X functions for fields and division
  rings.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YIf a field [3XF[103X is a subfield of a commutative ring [3XC[103X, [3XC[103X can be considered as a
  vector  space  over  the  (left)  acting  domain [3XF[103X (see ChapterÂ [14X61[114X). In this
  situation, we call [3XF[103X the [13Xfield of definition[113X of [3XC[103X.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YEach  field  in  [5XGAP[105X  is  represented  as  a  vector  space  over a subfield
  (seeÂ [2XIsField[102X  ([14X58.1-2[114X)),  thus  each field is in fact a field extension in a
  natural  way,  which  is  used  by functions such as [2XNorm[102X ([14X58.3-4[114X) and [2XTrace[102X
  ([14X58.3-5[114X) (seeÂ [14X58.3[114X).[133X

  [1X58.3 [33X[0;0YGalois Action[133X[101X
  
  [33X[0;0YLet [22XL > K[122X be a field extension of finite degree. Then to each element [22XÎ± â L[122X,
  we  can associate a [22XK[122X-linear mapping [22XÏ_Î±[122X on [22XL[122X, and for a fixed [22XK[122X-basis of [22XL[122X,
  we can associate to [22XÎ±[122X the matrix [22XM_Î±[122X (over [22XK[122X) of this mapping.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YThe  [13Xnorm[113X  of  [22XÎ±[122X  is  defined  as  the determinant of [22XM_Î±[122X, the [13Xtrace[113X of [22XÎ±[122X is
  defined  as  the  trace  of  [22XM_Î±[122X,  the  [13Xminimal polynomial[113X [22XÎ¼_Î±[122X and the [13Xtrace
  polynomial[113X  [22XÏ_Î±[122X  of [22XÎ±[122X are defined as the minimal polynomial (seeÂ [14X66.8-1[114X) and
  the  characteristic  polynomial  (seeÂ [2XCharacteristicPolynomial[102X ([14X24.13-1[114X) and
  [2XTracePolynomial[102X  ([14X58.3-3[114X))  of [22XM_Î±[122X. (Note that [22XÎ¼_Î±[122X depends only on [22XK[122X whereas
  [22XÏ_Î±[122X depends on both [22XL[122X and [22XK[122X.)[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YThus  norm and trace of [22XÎ±[122X are elements of [22XK[122X, and [22XÎ¼_Î±[122X and [22XÏ_Î±[122X are polynomials
  over [22XK[122X, [22XÏ_Î±[122X being a power of [22XÎ¼_Î±[122X, and the degree of [22XÏ_Î±[122X equals the degree of
  the field extension [22XL > K[122X.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YThe [13Xconjugates[113X of [22XÎ±[122X in [22XL[122X are those roots of [22XÏ_Î±[122X (with multiplicity) that lie
  in [22XL[122X; note that if only [22XL[122X is given, there is in general no way to access the
  roots outside [22XL[122X.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YAnalogously, the [13XGalois group[113X of the extension [22XL > K[122X is defined as the group
  of all those field automorphisms of [22XL[122X that fix [22XK[122X pointwise.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YIf [22XL > K[122X is a Galois extension then the conjugates of [22XÎ±[122X are all roots of [22XÏ_Î±[122X
  (with multiplicity), the set of conjugates equals the roots of [22XÎ¼_Î±[122X, the norm
  of  [22XÎ±[122X equals the product and the trace of [22XÎ±[122X equals the sum of the conjugates
  of  [22XÎ±[122X,  and the Galois group in the sense of the above definition equals the
  usual Galois group,[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YNote that [10XMinimalPolynomial( [3XF[103X[10X, [3Xz[103X[10X )[110X is a polynomial [13Xover[113X [3XF[103X, whereas [10XNorm( [3XF[103X[10X,
  [3Xz[103X[10X  )[110X  is  the  norm  of  the  element  [3Xz[103X [13Xin[113X [3XF[103X w.r.t.Â the field extension [10X[3XF[103X[10X >
  LeftActingDomain( [3XF[103X[10X )[110X.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YThe  default  methods  for  field elements are as follows. [2XMinimalPolynomial[102X
  ([14X66.8-1[114X)  solves  a  system  of  linear  equations, [2XTracePolynomial[102X ([14X58.3-3[114X)
