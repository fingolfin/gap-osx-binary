D.4.14 mprimdec_lib

Library:
mprimdec.lib
Purpose:
procedures for primary decomposition of modules
Authors:
Alexander Dreyer, dreyer@mathematik.uni-kl.de; adreyer@web.de

Overview:
Algorithms for primary decomposition for modules based on the algorithms of Gianni, Trager and Zacharias and
Shimoyama and Yokoyama (generalization of the latter suggested by Hans-Gert Graebe, Leipzig )
using elments of primdec.lib

Remark:
These procedures are implemented to be used in characteristic 0.
They also work in positive characteristic >> 0.
In small characteristic and for algebraic extensions, the procedures via Gianni, Trager, Zacharias may not terminate.

Procedures:

D.4.14.1 separator  computes a list of separators of prime ideals
D.4.14.2 PrimdecA  (not necessarily minimal) primary decomposition via Shimoyama/Yokoyama (suggested by Graebe)
D.4.14.3 PrimdecB  (not necessarily minimal) primary decomposition for pseudo-primary ideals
D.4.14.4 modDec  minimal primary decomposition via Shimoyama/Yokoyama (suggested by Graebe)
D.4.14.5 zeroMod  minimal zero-dimensional primary decomposition via Gianni, Trager and Zacharias
D.4.14.6 GTZmod  minimal primary decomposition via Gianni, Trager and Zacharias
D.4.14.7 dec1var  primary decomposition for one variable
D.4.14.8 annil  the annihilator of M/N in the basering
D.4.14.9 splitting  splitting to simpler modules
D.4.14.10 primTest  tests whether i is prime or homogeneous
D.4.14.11 preComp  enhanced Version of splitting
D.4.14.12 indSet  lists with varstrings of(in)dependend variables
D.4.14.13 GTZopt  a faster version of GTZmod
D.4.14.14 zeroOpt  a faster version of zeroMod
